TNRIS has a long history of successfully managing Lidar data acquisition projects. We have also helped guide data specifications for many other regional and local Lidar projects. TNRIS is now coordinating the acquistion of new, high quality elevation data for the 2016-17 winter season. This project supports regional and local governments by providing the opportunity to acquire these Lidar data products at significant cost savings.

This project will fall under the umbrella of the new Texas Strategic Mapping (StratMap) Contract, a GIS data and services contract held at the Department of Information Resources (DIR) and administered by TNRIS. The StratMap Contract is the second generation of geographic data contracts administered by TNRIS. It replaces the previous High Priority Imagery and Data Sets (HPIDS) Contract. Under the HPIDS Contract, TNRIS managed 19 Lidar acquisitions from 2009 to 2015 covering over 50,000 square miles in Texas.

Projects Developing Around the State

Area of Interest - AOI
Texas Water Development Board - TWDB

  • Funding Status: Committed
  • Area of Interest Status: Preliminary

The Texas Water Development Board has committed $900,000 to the acquisition of high resolution Lidar data for regions of central Texas in an effort to support floodplain management and planning.

These are the two main areas of interest for these funds, but only one of these areas is likely to be chosen for acquisition. Final AOI decisions will hinge on support and contribution from local and regional entities.

Area of Interest - AOI
San Antonio River Authority - SARA

  • Funding Status: Pending
  • Area of Interest Status: Preliminary

SARA is interested in an acquisition that would cover parts of their jurisdiction to support activities related to FEMA flood modeling and mapping, feature data extraction, water quality modeling, stream restoration potential analysis, and change detection. Additional local and regional partners are needed to secure funding and expand the area of interest. This AOI will also seek funding from the USGS through the 3DEP program.

Area of Interest - AOI
Williamson/Travis/Burnet County

  • Funding Status: Pending approval
  • Area of Interest Status: Preliminary

Williamson County

Williamson County is interested in acquiring high resolution Lidar data to be used in flood management as well as to assist in transportation planning.

Williamson County is looking for partners to expand the area of interest in an effort to create a regional dataset consisting of one data specification to improve regional elevation models. This acquisition will match the specification flown for the City of Georgetown in 2015 to create a seamless dataset using the State Lidar specification.

U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service

The United States Fish and Wildlife Service is obtaining data for projects evaluating woodland fuels important to mitigating hazardous wildfire conditions, and evaluating existing habitat conditions and improvement projects on the Balcones Canyonlands National Wildlife Refuge. Data will be used additionally to identify and model habitat for the endangered black-capped vireo, develop management prescriptions for black-capped vireo habitat, and develop fuels reduction treatments that are complementary with management of habitat for the threatened golden-cheeked warbler.
